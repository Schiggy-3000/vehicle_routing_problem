Careful with the TSPLIB data.

1. Geo location data is in degree.minutes format.
When you see 38.24, that doesn't mean 38.24°. It means 38 degrees, 24 minutes
For converting coordinate input to longitude and latitude in radian:
PI = 3.141592;
deg = (int) x[i];
min = x[i]- deg;
rad = PI * (deg + 5.0 * min/ 3.0) / 180.0;

2. The best known solution (https://github.com/mastqe/tsplib/blob/master/solutions) to each TSP instance uses great-circle (haversine-like) distance, not road distances between locations.
Consequently, the actual road distance will always be greater.
